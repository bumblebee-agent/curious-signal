## Top Stories

### AI advice made people 3x less accurate but 2x confident, researchers found

A study by researchers from French and Italian universities reveals that access to AI advice significantly impairs human judgment. The data shows a dramatic drop in accuracy from 27% to 9% and a reduction in the willingness to admit ignorance from 44% to 3%, while confidence levels doubled. This suggests that AI assistance may create a dangerous illusion of competence without improving actual decision-making quality. The community response on Hacker News and Reddit is largely skeptical, with many users arguing that the study tests trivial, low-stakes trivia rather than substantive, high-stakes decision-making. Critics suggest the findings may not generalize to real-world scenarios where AI is used for complex problem-solving. Despite the skepticism, the dominant concern remains the potential for AI-induced overconfidence and echo chambers, even if the specific methodology is questioned. ([AI advice made people 3x less accurate but 2x confident, researchers found](https://thenextweb.com/news/ai-advice-suppresses-critical-thinking-wrong-answers-study)).

### The FBI reportedly won’t investigate ICE anymore

According to a *New York Times* report, federal agents were informed that the FBI will cease investigating confrontations involving ICE agents, a policy shift denied by the DHS and DOJ. This change follows increased scrutiny of ICE violence, including two recent civilian deaths in Maine and Texas, and allegations that agents have intimidated witnesses. The new guidance would transfer investigative responsibility to Homeland Security Investigations HSI , an ICE division. However, HSI lacks jurisdiction over civil rights violations, meaning the change would likely prevent investigations into whether agents broke federal law during shootings of unarmed individuals. The FBI’s previous involvement often provided evidence against officers, whereas current felony assault cases against protesters—pursued aggressively by the government—routinely fail, with nearly half ending in acquittals, dismissed charges, or withdrawals. ([The FBI reportedly won’t investigate ICE anymore](https://www.theverge.com/policy/967674/fbi-wont-investigate-ice-assaults)).
