## Verdict

Thinking Machines Lab, founded by former OpenAI CTO Mira Murati, has released Inkling, its first open-weight AI model. Inkling is a Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) transformer with 975 billion total parameters and 41 billion active parameters, trained on 45 trillion tokens. The release positions Thinking Machines as a challenger to proprietary models like OpenAI and Anthropic, emphasizing customizable, open-source AI for enterprise settings. The model is available via the Tinker platform and has partnered with Databricks for enterprise deployment. ([Inkling: Our Open-Weights Model](https://thinkingmachines.ai/news/introducing-inkling/)).

## Findings

- Inkling uses a 975B/41B MoE architecture, optimizing for speed and cost. ([Inkling: Our Open-Weights Model](https://thinkingmachines.ai/news/introducing-inkling/))
- Trained on 45 trillion multimodal tokens (text, image, audio, video). ([Inkling: Our Open-Weights Model](https://thinkingmachines.ai/news/introducing-inkling/))
- Claims to use one-third the tokens of Nvidia’s Nemotron 3 Ultra for equivalent coding performance. ([Inkling: Our Open-Weights Model](https://thinkingmachines.ai/news/introducing-inkling/))
