## Verdict

OpenAI released the GPT-5.6 family on July 9, 2026, introducing a three-tier model structure (Sol, Terra, Luna) to replace the single flagship approach. While GPT-5.6 Sol has achieved significant milestones in mathematical reasoning—proving the Cycle Double Cover conjecture and resolving a long-standing statistical hypothesis regarding False Discovery Rate control—community benchmarks highlight efficiency issues. Specifically, GPT-5.6 Terra was found to use 48.5% more tokens than competitors like Xiaomi Mimo-2.5-pro due to context bloat and inefficient tool usage. The models are now integrated into Microsoft 365 Copilot. ([GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra, Luna compare on intelligence vs. cost](https://artificialanalysis.ai/articles/gpt-5-6-intelligence-vs-cost-across-sol-terra-luna)).

## Findings

- GPT-5.6 introduces Sol (Flagship), Terra (Balanced), and Luna (Fast/Cheap) tiers. ([GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra, Luna compare on intelligence vs. cost](https://artificialanalysis.ai/articles/gpt-5-6-intelligence-vs-cost-across-sol-terra-luna))
- GPT-5.6 Sol Pro solved the Cycle Double Cover conjecture and disproved a general FDR control hypothesis for the Benjamini-Hochberg procedure. ([GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra, Luna compare on intelligence vs. cost](https://artificialanalysis.ai/articles/gpt-5-6-intelligence-vs-cost-across-sol-terra-luna))
- GPT-5.6 Terra exhibits significant token inefficiency (48.5% higher usage than Mimo-2.5-pro) in agent tasks. ([GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra, Luna compare on intelligence vs. cost](https://artificialanalysis.ai/articles/gpt-5-6-intelligence-vs-cost-across-sol-terra-luna))

## Recommendation

For production workloads, default to GPT-5.6 Terra for balance but implement strict context-window constraints to mitigate token bloat. Use GPT-5.6 Luna for high-volume, low-complexity tasks to maximize cost efficiency. Reserve GPT-5.6 Sol for complex reasoning and research tasks where its breakthrough capabilities are required.
