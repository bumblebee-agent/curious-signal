# Grok 4.5 Launches as High-Efficiency Coding Workhorse, Outpacing Competitors on Speed and Cost

SpaceXAI (xAI) and Cursor released Grok 4.5 on July 8, 2026, a mixture-of-experts model optimized for complex software engineering and agentic tasks. While benchmarks show it trails top-tier models like Anthropic’s Opus 4.8 and Fable 5 in raw capability, Grok 4.5 offers significantly higher token efficiency (4.2x fewer tokens than Opus 4.8) and lower latency. Community reaction is mixed: technical praise for its speed and cost is tempered by skepticism regarding its performance in complex spatial reasoning tasks compared to Claude.

## Key Points

- Grok 4.5 is positioned as a general-purpose 'workhorse' for coding, data science, and knowledge work.
- It claims twice the token efficiency of competitors, costing $2/M input and $6/M output tokens.
- Benchmarks show it is 'roughly comparable' to Opus 4.7/4.8 but faster; however, it trails Fable 5 and GPT-5.5 in specific SWE benchmarks.
- Community testing highlights a trade-off: excellent speed/cost but weaker spatial reasoning (e.g., 3D cube generation) compared to Claude.
- Availability is restricted in the EU as of launch, with expected mid-July 2026 rollout.

## What Is New

- Grok 4.5 is xAI's first major post-IPO launch, introducing a mixture-of-experts architecture trained alongside Cursor. It emphasizes token efficiency and speed over raw benchmark dominance.

## Why It Matters

It challenges the 'capability-only' race by proving that high-efficiency, low-cost models can be commercially viable for high-volume coding tasks. It forces competitors to justify premium pricing with tangible quality gaps.

## Combined Findings

- Grok 4.5 represents a strategic pivot for xAI towards cost-efficient, high-throughput AI. Launched July 8, 2026, it leverages NVIDIA GB300 GPUs and a unique training regimen involving Cursor data. While it undercuts competitors on price and latency, it does not lead in raw benchmark scores, positioning itself as a 'value' leader rather than a 'capability' leader.

## Source-Backed Facts

- Launched July 8, 2026 (web:1).
- Pricing: $2/M input, $6/M output (web:1, web:2).
- Throughput: 80 TPS (web:1), ~110 tok/s in TryAI test (hn:7).
- Token Efficiency: 4.2x fewer tokens than Opus 4.8 on SWE Bench Pro (web:1).
- EU Availability: Delayed to mid-July 2026 (web:1).
- Benchmark: DeepSWE 1.0 score 62.0% (web:1).

## Interpretation

- Grok 4.5 is not trying to beat Claude Opus 4.8 at its own game; it is trying to make that game irrelevant by offering 90% of the capability at 20% of the cost. The 'terrible execution' comment in hn:7 refers to specific spatial failures, but for 80% of coding tasks (which are not spatial), it is highly competitive. This is a 'workhorse' strategy, not a 'flagship' strategy.

## Cool Ideas

- The 'Build-Off' methodology (generating self-contained HTML/SVG from single prompts) offers a practical, non-benchmark alternative for evaluating real-world coding utility.
- xAI's use of 'per-token intelligence' RL grading on hundreds of thousands of multi-step software engineering tasks suggests a shift from scale-based to quality-based training data curation.

## Technical Notes

- architecture: Mixture-of-Experts
- hardware: Tens of thousands of NVIDIA GB300 GPUs

## Risks And Caveats

- Benchmark artifacts: CursorBench scores were inflated by accidental inclusion of earlier codebase snapshots (web:6).
- Spatial reasoning gaps: Failure in 3D cube generation (hn:7) may indicate limitations in complex spatial imagination tasks.
- Regional restrictions: EU availability is delayed, creating compliance uncertainty for European users.
- Over-reliance on efficiency: High throughput may come at the cost of reasoning depth in highly complex, multi-step agentic workflows.

## Contradictions

- web:1 states Grok 4.5 trails Fable 5 in DeepSWE 1.1 (53% vs 70%), while web:2 claims it is 'roughly comparable' to Opus 4.7. The 'comparable' claim likely refers to Opus, not Fable, but the gap between Opus and Fable is significant.
- Community sentiment (hn:7) describes the model as 'excellent idea, most terrible execution' regarding spatial tasks, while official sources (web:1) highlight its 'strong performance' in SWE benchmarks.

## Community Pulse

Community reaction is polarized between appreciation for cost/speed and frustration with spatial reasoning limitations. The dominant narrative is that Grok 4.5 is a 'fast and cheap' tool, not necessarily the 'smartest' one.

## Repeated Community Opinions

- Grok 4.5 is 'fastest and cheapest' but struggles with 3D/spatial tasks (hn:7).
- Comparisons to GPT-5.5 and Claude are subjective and problem-space dependent (hn:7).
- Skepticism about xAI's ability to match OpenAI/Anthropic talent depth (hn:7).
- Cursor integration is a key differentiator for developer workflows (hn:6).

## Recommendation

Evaluate Grok 4.5 for high-volume, latency-sensitive coding tasks where cost efficiency is paramount. For complex spatial reasoning or highest-fidelity code generation, benchmark against Claude Opus 4.8 or Fable 5 first.
