## Verdict

Grok 4.5 is a workhorse strategy: deliver enough capability for serious coding and agentic work, then compete aggressively on speed and cost. The [xAI release](https://x.ai/news/grok-4-5) reports 80 tokens per second and pricing of $2 per million input tokens and $6 per million output tokens.

That positioning matters because many production workloads are constrained less by the final benchmark point than by latency, token use, and how many tasks can be completed within budget.

## Findings

- xAI positions the model for coding, data science, research, and general knowledge work ([xAI](https://x.ai/news/grok-4-5)).
- Cursor describes joint development and training environments based on developer-agent interactions ([Cursor](https://cursor.com/blog/grok-4-5)).
- xAI reports using 4.2 times fewer tokens than Opus 4.8 on SWE-Bench Pro, although this remains a vendor comparison ([xAI](https://x.ai/news/grok-4-5)).
- An independent three-task build comparison found strong speed and cost but weaker spatial execution in one 3D task ([TryAI](https://www.tryai.dev/blog/grok-4.5-vs-gpt-5.5-vs-claude-build-off)).
- Market coverage emphasizes the price gap against premium competitors ([SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/07/08/spacexais-newest-ai-model-grok-4-5-dramatically-undercuts-anthropic-openai-price/)).

## Risks

- Self-reported benchmark and efficiency claims need independent replication.
- Cursor disclosed an artifact affecting an earlier CursorBench comparison ([Cursor](https://cursor.com/blog/grok-4-5)).
- Small build-offs are useful demonstrations, but they cannot establish general model quality.
- Spatial and visual reasoning may vary sharply from ordinary software-engineering performance.
- Regional availability was constrained at launch according to the official announcement.

## Recommendation

Benchmark Grok 4.5 on high-volume, latency-sensitive coding tasks where cost is measurable. Keep a stronger premium model in the comparison for difficult architecture, visual, or spatial work. The right decision is likely task routing, not a single permanent winner.

### Selected Reported Results

- DeepSWE 1.0: 62.0% in xAI's published comparison.
- Terminal Bench 2.1: 83.3% in xAI's published comparison.
- SWE-Bench Pro: 64.7% in xAI's published comparison.
- Throughput: 80 tokens per second according to xAI; roughly 110 tokens per second in the small TryAI test.

### Open Questions

- How does the model hold up in long-running, multi-turn agent workflows?
- Does its token efficiency survive repository-scale tasks with repeated tool use?
- Which workloads expose the spatial reasoning weakness seen in the small build comparison?
- How much do corrected benchmark artifacts change its relative position?

Confidence is medium: the primary sources are detailed but promotional, and the independent testing sample remains small.
