## Verdict

Black Forest Labs (BFL) has introduced FLUX 3, a unified multimodal foundation model capable of generating images, 20-second video clips with synchronized native audio, and robotic action predictions from a single prompt. Unlike competitors using separate models, FLUX 3 employs a 'Self-Flow' architecture trained jointly across modalities. The release is currently in 'Early Access' with no public API or open weights yet. Preliminary benchmarks suggest it outperforms key rivals like Luma Ray 3.2 and Runway Gen-4.5 in human preference tests. ([Flux 3: One multi-modal model for Image, Video, Audio and Action-Prediction](https://bfl.ai/blog/flux-3)).

## Findings

- Unified Architecture: Jointly learns from images, video, and audio using the 'Self-Flow' approach. ([Flux 3: One multi-modal model for Image, Video, Audio and Action-Prediction](https://bfl.ai/blog/flux-3))
- Video & Audio: Generates up to 20-second clips with native audio; supports text-to-video, image-to-video, and agentic chaining. ([Flux 3: One multi-modal model for Image, Video, Audio and Action-Prediction](https://bfl.ai/blog/flux-3))
- Robotics: 'FLUX-mimic' integrates with Mimic Robotics, reducing task-specific training data needs from 30+ hours to 30 minutes. ([Flux 3: One multi-modal model for Image, Video, Audio and Action-Prediction](https://bfl.ai/blog/flux-3))
